Why Most Global Brands
Fail in New Markets

Nike sells motivation, not sneakers. Apple sells creativity, not computers. The product is just the thing you hand someone. The real sale happens when they see themselves differently after buying it.

I've watched international hospitality brands try to enter the Gulf and India using the exact same pitch they use in Europe or the US. Same brochures. Same positioning. Same failure rate. The problem isn't that their properties are bad—it's that nobody's explaining why a buyer in Dubai should care.

A Maldives resort means different things depending on who's buying. A tour operator in Dubai sees group bookings and commission structures. An agent in Mumbai sees honeymoon packages and family holidays. A corporate buyer in Riyadh sees executive retreats. Same resort, three completely different conversations.

That's what we actually do. We don't just carry your brand deck to meetings. We translate what you're selling into what the local market is buying. Most firms understand one side or the other. We've spent fifteen years working both, so we know how to connect them.

Ram Kumar
Founder & CEO, Koonafa Global
